Tarmac Restoration

Tarmac is one of the cheapest surfaces to lay and can be very durable if laid correctly. However, over time, the surface will dry out due to weathering and the effects of UV light from the sun. If this situation continues for a long time, the surface may start to crumble and crack.

There are many tarmac driveways across Leicestershire and Nottinghamshire, as well as commercial premises that have tarmac surfaces. Many of these surfaces are in need of general refurbishment, but not many people know how to go about this and consequently the surfaces are often left until they have deteriorated completely.

Completely replacing the tarmac driveway can be expensive and is often unnecessary. As long as the surface is still intact, the application of Tarmaseal™ Tarmac Restorer will put the lost resins back into the surface and completely transform the condition and look of it. Small cracks can be repaired by using a Crack Repair Compound.

This replacing of lost resins will also give the driveway long-lasting protection against future deterioration.

The whole process can usually be completed in a day on a domestic property. It will involve lightly pressure washing the surface and after it has dried fully, applying Tarmaseal™ with a roller.
